Papa Imposter

Welcome to our Rube Goldberg machine!

About the Project

The Papa Imposter Rube Goldberg Project is a machine thats feeds a pet some pizza. In the end the biggest marble knocks down the support for the pizza, so the bottom part of the box falls down. This causes the pizza box to open allowing a pet to eat it. The theme of our Rube Goldberg machine is Among Us. This is the theme because, to start the imposter gets ejected into space with some stars in the background. In our project a marble representing the imposter gets launched into a blanket with stars on its background. This Rube Goldberg machine also has many energy transformations. The energy lost is very minimal. Looking at the difference in energies, we can see that the energy lost is very minimal, which was mainly due to some very minimal friction and sound. The difference is only \(0.001 \mathrm{J}\), which is very tiny, meaning that this machine conserves energy well. Now, is this a good thing or not? The point of a Rube Goldberg machine is for it to be over-the-top and take a lot of time and energy for it to function, and do the task that could’ve been done with less energy, less energy loss, and less time. This Rube Goldberg machine that we’ve built does this with minimal energy loss, takes a moderate amount of time, and doesn’t require much energy. So, from a Rube Goldberg standpoint, the \(0.001 \mathrm{J}\) loss and \(6.08\, \mathrm{s}\) time is a bad thing, because it is not losing enough, but from an efficiency standpoint, it is effective, with minimal losses, minimal time, and minimal initial energy needed. To improve the efficiency of the machine, remove the cardboard and use a more smooth surface that would cause less friction, which would keep the energy the same.

How It Works

  • Step 1: The small marble get launched by a Catapult.
  • Step 2: The marble hits the blanket and falls down through a tunnel towards the Dominos.
  • Step 3: The Dominos get hit each other and get taller each time.
  • Step 4: The tallest Domino hits a big marble and it rolls down a ramp.
  • Step 5: The marble rolls off the ramp and hits a counterweight
  • Step 6: The counterweight falls and the pizza box opens in front of the pet.

Energy Transformations

Here are the transformations in energy throughout our machine.

  1. \(EPE \rightarrow KE\) - when the ball launched from the catapult
  2. \(KE \rightarrow GPE\) - when the ball lands on the platform
  3. \(GPE \rightarrow KE\) - when the ball funnels through
  4. \(KE \rightarrow TE\) - some energy lost through friction which generates heat
  5. \(KE \rightarrow SE\) - when the dominos hit each other, it generates some sound
  6. \(GPE \rightarrow KE\) - when the ball gets hit by the big domino, it starts to move
  7. \(GPE \rightarrow KE\) - when the rod holding up the pizza gets knocked, the bottom of the box falls
  8. \(GPE \rightarrow KE\) - when the ball goes down the ramp and hits the other balls, they start to move
  9. \(KE \rightarrow SE\) - When the catapult launches, it hits the table, creating sound
  10. \(KE \rightarrow TE\) - When the end ball hits the rod off and hits the table, it loses all the KE to friction, which is thermal energy.

    \(EPE\) stands for Elastic Potential Energy.
    \(KE\) stands for Kinetic Energy.
    \(GPE\) stands for Gravitational Potential Energy.
    \(TE\) stands for Thermal Energy
    \(SE\) stands for Sound Energy

Calculations

We used a meter stick to measure the heights for the gravitational potential energy (\(GPE\)), which had no kinetic energy (\(KE\)) at the time, meaning that was all the energy. We used a scale to measure the weights (\(m\)). We used the equation \(GPE = m \cdot g \cdot h\) to figure out the \(GPE\), which was the mechanical energy (\(ME\)) because of the reasoning before. To find when it was moving, we found the height (\(h\)). The height was on the table, so it was \(0\, \mathrm{m}\). Using the potential energies (\(PE_{\text{before}}\) and \(PE_{\text{after}}\)), we subtracted them to find the work (\(W\)) applied by friction, sound, and more: \(W = PE_{\text{before}} - PE_{\text{after}}\). Using this, we calculated the final \(PE\) because we knew that no energy was lost between them, as dominos do not have any loss, other than very minimal air resistance. Finally, the power calculations were done with the equation \(P = \frac{W}{t}\) where \(P\) is power in watts, \(W\) is work in joules, and \(t\) is time in seconds.

Energy

  1. Height = \(19\, \mathrm{cm}\) - start of the end ramp (\(GPE\))

    \[ GPE = m \cdot g \cdot h \\ GPE = (0.19\, \mathrm{m}) \cdot (9.81\, \mathrm{m/s^2}) \cdot (0.101\, \mathrm{kg}) \\ GPE = 0.188\, \mathrm{J} \]

  2. Height = \(28\, \mathrm{cm}\) - lands on cardboard after blanket (\(GPE\))

    \[ GPE = m \cdot g \cdot h \\ GPE = (0.28\, \mathrm{m}) \cdot (9.81\, \mathrm{m/s^2}) \cdot (0.068\, \mathrm{kg}) \\ GPE = 0.187\, \mathrm{J} \]

  3. Height = \(0\, \mathrm{cm}\) - On the ground rolling (\(KE\))

    \[ GPE + W = KE \\ KE = 0.187\, \mathrm{J} + 0\, \mathrm{J} \\ KE = 0.187\, \mathrm{J} \]

    The marbles experience no friction, and if any occurs, it is a negligible amount, which is reasonable.

Work

    We calculated work from before the catapult launched the smaller marble to when the big marble is at the end of the ramp.

\[ W + PE_i + KE_i = PE_f + KE_f \\ W + 0\, \mathrm{J} + 0\, \mathrm{J} = 0.094\, \mathrm{J} + 0.94\, \mathrm{J} \\ W = 0.188\, \mathrm{J} \\ d = 0.185\, \mathrm{m} \\ \frac{W}{d} = N \\ N = \frac{0.188\, \mathrm{J}}{0.185\, \mathrm{J}} \\ N = 1.0162\, \mathrm{N} \]

Power

    We calculated power from the same part of the machine as the work, which is why we used the same work value as above.

\[ P = \frac{W}{t} \\ P = \frac{1.0162\, \mathrm{N}}{6.08\, \mathrm{s}} \\ P = 0.16713\, \mathrm{W} \]
